About

The annual film festival is open to students in years 7-12 and features nine film categories. The festival takes place on May 22nd, and the submission deadline is May 15th. All submissions must be 3-minute-long original works, respectful of UAE laws, and not harming the environment or animals. Additionally, each submission must include a brief synopsis, a list of credits, a 10-second trailer, and a passport-size picture with a white background. The top five finalists in each category will be notified by email. The categories include Wes Anderson-inspired theme, Silent Film, Animation, Hybrid Genre, Student Life, Horror, Diversity and Inclusion, Fantasy, and True Representation. Each category has specific requirements for inspiration, characters, visual style, and storytelling, which must be considered for the film to qualify.

1. Wes Anderson inspired theme.
Requirements:

Inspiration: The film must be inspired by the works of Wes Anderson, but not a direct copy of any of his films. Use his style, quirks, and unique visual aesthetic as inspiration.

Characters: The film must include at least one quirky or eccentric character. This can be the main character or a supporting character.

Visual Style: The film should have a distinctive visual style that is like Wes Anderson's films. You can use his signature colors, camera angles, symmetry and set designs as inspiration.

Story: The film should have a clear and compelling story that engages the audience. This can be any genre or style of story, as long as it is well-told and interesting.

2. Silent films
Films that rely on visuals and sound effects rather than dialogue, showcasing the power of visual storytelling.

Requirements:

Inspiration: The film can draw inspiration from any era or style of silent film but should aim to create a fresh take on the genre.

Characters: The film must feature strong and expressive characters that are capable of conveying emotions and thoughts without the use of dialogue.

Visual Style: The visual style of the film should be a key component of the storytelling process, with strong use of contrast, framing, and lighting. It can be inspired by any era or style of silent film but should aim to be visually striking and captivating. The use of intertitles or text overlays can be used sparingly to convey necessary information.

3. Animation
Animated films that experiment with techniques such as stop-motion or pixelation etc. to create unique and creative visuals.

Requirements:

Inspiration: Entrants are free to draw inspiration from any theme, subject matter, or genre, but must showcase creative and original storytelling.

Characters: The film must include at least one unique and well-developed character that adds depth and complexity to the story.

Visual Style: The film should have a visually distinctive style that showcases the entrant's technical skills and creative vision. Color, texture, and camera angles should enhance the storytelling and create an immersive experience.

4. Hybrid genre
Films that blend two or more film genres in a creative and unexpected way, creating a new and unique cinematic experience.

Requirements:

Inspiration: The film should be inspired by at least two different film genres and blend them in a creative and unexpected way, creating a unique cinematic experience.

Characters: The film should include characters that fit into both genres and their respective tropes, creating a cohesive and engaging storyline.

Visual Style: The film should have a visual style that reflects both genres and their respective visual aesthetics, creating a cohesive and visually stunning experience for the audience.

Story: The film should have a clear and engaging story that blends the two genres seamlessly, with a compelling plot that keeps the audience engaged throughout.

5. Student life films
Films that explore the experiences, challenges, and joys of being a student, in high school or college.

Requirements:

Inspiration: The film must be inspired by real-life experiences of being a student in high school or college. It should capture the challenges, joys, and complexities of this phase of life. The film can take inspiration from personal anecdotes, observations, or broader themes that are commonly associated with student life.

Characters: The film must include relatable and diverse student characters that audiences can connect with. These characters can be based on real-life individuals or archetypes that are commonly associated with student life. It's important to showcase the different experiences and perspectives that students bring to the table.

Visual Style: The film should have a distinctive visual style that complements the themes and mood of the story. This can include the use of colors, camera angles, lighting, and other cinematic techniques that evoke the feeling of being a student. The visual style should be consistent with the tone and message of the film.

6. Horror
Requirements:

Inspiration: The film must be inspired by classic kid-friendly horror stories such as Goosebumps or Scary Stories to Tell in the Dark. Use spooky but not overly scary elements to create a suspenseful and thrilling story that will keep the audience on the edge of their seats.

Characters: The film must include a range of likable and relatable characters, including both protagonists and antagonists. The main character should be brave and resourceful, facing challenges and overcoming obstacles in their quest to defeat the monster or solve the mystery.

Visual Style: The film should have a visually appealing and creative style that enhances the horror elements without being too graphic or violent. Use lighting, camera angles, and sound effects to create an atmosphere of mystery and suspense. The setting should be eerie and mysterious, full of hidden clues and secrets. Make use of fun and imaginative scares, creepy creatures, and other classic horror elements that will appeal to kids without being too frightening.

7. Diversity and inclusion films
Films that explore issues of diversity, equity, and inclusion, highlighting the experiences of people from different backgrounds and identities.

Requirements:

Inspiration: Films should aim to explore issues of diversity, equity, and inclusion, highlighting the experiences of people from different backgrounds and identities. These films should be inspired by real-life stories and experiences.

Characters: Films should feature a diverse range of characters, highlighting different experiences, identities, and perspectives. These characters can be the main focus or supporting characters, but they should be authentic and well-developed.

Visual Style: Films should utilize a visual style that is both engaging and impactful, drawing the audience into the stories being told. This can include the use of color, lighting, camera angles, and other visual elements that add to the emotional impact of the film.

8. Fantasy films
Films that explore fantastical and imaginary worlds, showcasing the power of imagination and creativity.

Requirements:

Inspiration: The film must draw inspiration from elements of fantasy, such as mythical creatures, magical powers, or other supernatural phenomena. It should showcase the power of imagination and creativity in building fantastical and imaginary worlds.

Characters: The film must include compelling and memorable characters that fit within the fantasy genre, such as wizards, dragons, fairies, or other mythical beings. These characters can be the main protagonist or supporting characters that add depth to the story.

Visual Style: The film should have a distinctive visual style that captures the fantastical elements of the story. This can include elaborate costumes, intricate set designs, and special effects that help bring the imaginary world to life.

9. True Representation
Requirements:

Inspiration: The film should be inspired by real-life stories or experiences to accurately represent diverse cultures, identities, and perspectives. It should also be inclusive of individuals from different backgrounds and communities.

Characters: The film should feature a diverse range of characters that are authentic and fully realized, with unique personalities, strengths, and flaws. These characters should be depicted in a way that accurately represents their experiences and backgrounds.

Visual Style: The film should use visual elements to enhance the message of true representation. This could include choosing a particular color palette, lighting, or camera angles that capture the essence of the story and the characters' experiences.
